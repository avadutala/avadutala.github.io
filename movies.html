Stories on screen

Movies I watched, remembered, and carried with me.

Some films stay with me because of music. Some because of family. Some because they show courage in dark times. And some because they open the door to science fiction, imagination, and impossible questions.

Movies that stayed with me

These are not just titles on a list. Each one connects to a memory, an emotion, a phase of life, or a way of thinking.

All-time favorite

Sakhi

My all-time favorite. It is a film I return to for its warmth, music, emotion, and the way it captures love as something personal, imperfect, and deeply human.

Mani Ratnam

Nayakudu

A rare film with emotional force, character depth, and unforgettable storytelling. It feels like the kind of cinema that cannot be casually repeated, even by a master filmmaker.

Closer to the heart

Dil Se

A movie that feels closer to the heart than words can easily explain. Its music, intensity, and emotional restlessness stay long after the film ends.

Science fiction and family

Interstellar

For me, this is not only a space movie. The father-daughter relationship is the center of its power, and that emotional thread makes the science feel personal.

History and humanity

Schindler’s List

This was the first of many World War-era films I watched during my Sweden education days. It led me to explore more stories about concentration camps, survival, cruelty, and moral courage.

Difficult and powerful

The Grey Zone

Depressingly beautiful. A heavy film that does not make survival look simple, and that is what makes it so powerful and hard to forget.

Hope in darkness

Life Is Beautiful

A beautiful and painful story about love, imagination, and protection. It shows how humor and tenderness can exist even in the darkest parts of history.

Mind and resilience

A Beautiful Mind

A moving story about intelligence, struggle, love, and endurance. It reminds me that brilliance and vulnerability can live inside the same person.

Music and memory

Nireekshana

For me, Ilaiyaraaja is the god of music. The film stays in memory because the music gives the emotions a life of their own.

Science fiction

I am always interested in science fiction — space, time, alien life, intelligence, technology, future worlds, and stories that push imagination beyond ordinary life.

War and history

I am drawn to films that show moral complexity, survival, cruelty, courage, and the difficult parts of human history without making them easy.

Music and emotion

Some films stay with me because of songs, background scores, silences, love, memory, and the emotional world they create.

Movies are another way of traveling — into someone else’s memory, fear, courage, love, or imagination.
