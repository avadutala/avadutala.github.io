Places I have lived in or visited

Vizag, India

Home town

This is where everything began. I was born and raised in Vizag, and my memories of home include childhood, family, the sea, familiar streets, and my pet dog Bubblu.

Hyderabad, India

Biryani city

Hyderabad will always be connected to biryani for me. It is one of my favorite foods, and Hyderabad feels like the natural home for that love.

Bangalore, India

First job

Bangalore was where my working life began. It also gave me memories of unlimited buffets and my introduction to Black Forest ice cream.

Chennai, India

Marriage, temples, beaches, food

Chennai holds the early days of marriage, temple visits, beaches, Anjappar, and many food memories. It is a city tied to a personal chapter, not just a place on a map.

Pune, India

Last Indian city before moving abroad

Pune was my last city in India before moving abroad. I remember it for good food, movies, and the feeling of standing just before a major change in life.

Karlskrona, Sweden

First international home

Karlskrona was my first international city and country during my master’s education. It gave me my first real glimpse of snow and the feeling of being far from home in a completely new world.

Gothenburg, Sweden

Visited

I visited Gothenburg during my Sweden chapter. It did not become one of my favorite places, but travel also teaches us that not every city connects with us in the same way.

Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia

Petronas Towers and local food

Kuala Lumpur gave me my first view of the Petronas Towers. I also spent a year eating in local restaurants nearby, which made the city part of my food memories.

Petaling Jaya, Malaysia

Work and daily life

Petaling Jaya was where I worked and lived for more than a year. It represents the daily rhythm of life in Malaysia beyond the famous landmarks.

Langkawi, Malaysia

Beautiful island

Langkawi was a short stay, but a beautiful one. A few days there were enough to leave a peaceful island memory.

Bangkok, Thailand

Family, food, and entrepreneurship

Bangkok is one of the most important cities in my life. My second daughter was born there, my first daughter learned Thai and spoke it, and I lived there for five years. It is also where Thai food became a permanent addiction for me.

Phuket, Thailand

World-class beaches

Phuket means long vacations, world-class beaches, and time spent slowing down near the water. It is one of those places that naturally feels like a holiday.

Toronto, Canada

First North American city

Toronto was my first North American city. I arrived there without a job, which made the experience uncertain, challenging, and unforgettable.

London, Ontario

First international job

London, Ontario gave me my first job internationally. That made it an important milestone in my professional life abroad.

Windsor, Canada

First house in Canada

Windsor became the first place I truly liked after my hometown. It gave me great friends, a huge backyard, snow shoveling, lawn mowing, my first Tesla, and the beginning of a very good chapter of life.

Detroit, USA

Work across the border

Detroit was my first real glimpse of the United States. I worked at Chrysler there while living in Canada, crossing into a different country for work.

Phoenix, USA

Desert life and tennis

I drove from Canada to Phoenix and saw giant cactus for the first time. I had no idea cactus could be that big, and I was genuinely excited. Phoenix brought Nikola, desert heat, year-round tennis, food everywhere, and Mexico within reach. In many ways, it feels like heaven.

Las Vegas, Los Angeles, San Diego, Grand Canyon, Flagstaff

Southwest memories

The Southwest gave me many road trips: Las Vegas, Los Angeles, San Diego, repeated visits to the Grand Canyon, and my first time skiing in Flagstaff.

Rocky Point / Puerto Peñasco, Mexico

Beach time

Rocky Point became an easy beach escape near the Mexico border, a place to spend good time by the water without traveling too far.

Hawaii

Simply wow

Hawaii was simply wow. Jurassic Park-like forests, beautiful food, clean beaches, and passion fruits made it feel almost unreal.

Places I want to visit

Japan

My daughters love sushi, so Japan feels like the perfect place to experience it at the source. I want to go for the food, the culture, and the precision in everyday life.

Australia

A continent-country with animals found almost nowhere else in the world. That alone makes me want to visit, explore, and see a different kind of nature.

Europe by road

A long-time dream is to drive through Europe slowly, crossing countries by road and experiencing the change in food, language, architecture, and landscape.

China

I love food, and China is one of the countries I want to visit for that reason alone. I also want to see the scale, history, and everyday life there.

Middle East

Food is a big reason again, but so are markets, deserts, history, hospitality, and the feeling of standing where many old cultures meet.

Brazil

The Amazon forest is the main pull. I spent my childhood around forests, and I want to see what the world’s biggest forest feels like in person.
